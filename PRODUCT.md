# Reggie Alleyne portfolio product context

## Register

Brand. This portfolio is a public-facing proof surface for Reggie Alleyne's product, design, and AI implementation work. The design is part of the product: visitors are judging taste, judgment, clarity, and follow-through before they read every case study.

## Audience

- Hiring managers and recruiters looking for product design, frontend, or AI product work.
- Founders and operators who need someone who can turn rough product intent into a working interface.
- Technical reviewers who want to see implementation judgment, not only polished screenshots.

## Purpose

The site should help a visitor answer three questions quickly:

1. What kind of problems does Reggie work on?
2. What decisions did he make, and why?
3. Is there enough proof to trust the work?

## Voice

Clear, specific, and slightly editorial. The copy should sound like a working designer explaining the decisions behind the artifact. It should avoid inflated claims, fake metrics, and portfolio language that makes every project sound world-changing.

Preferred copy shape:

- Problem, constraint, decision, proof.
- Short headings that name the actual thing.
- Evidence copy that sounds grounded enough for a reviewer to inspect.

Avoid:

- "Elevates", "empowers", "seamless", "groundbreaking", or "pivotal".
- Thesis-style case-study titles.
- Unsupported launch, revenue, adoption, or performance claims.
- Generic "AI-powered" language without naming the behavior or workflow.

## Strategic principles

- Lead with product judgment before decoration.
- Keep case-study evidence close to the card or section that makes the claim.
- Treat Staybooked as the strongest current project-detail reference.
- Keep Work as the primary public portfolio surface. Playground is for prototypes and experiments.
- Use generated visuals as art-direction and guide assets unless a later pass explicitly selects them for the live site.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards.
- Purple glass cards repeated as the whole identity.
- Endless equal-card grids.
- Side-stripe proof callouts.
- Portfolio copy that reads like a whitepaper abstract.
